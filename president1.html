During his presidency, George Washington established many foundational aspects of the federal government. He formed the first Cabinet and supported Alexander Hamilton’s financial plans, including creating the First Bank of the United States. Washington also dealt with domestic challenges like the Whiskey Rebellion, where he personally led troops to suppress the uprising against a federal excise tax on whiskey.


In foreign affairs, he maintained neutrality during the French Revolutionary Wars and secured important treaties, such as the Jay Treaty with Great Britain. He chose not to seek a third term and delivered a Farewell Address in 1796, warning against political parties and foreign alliances. His leadership laid the foundation for the United States’ future governance.
